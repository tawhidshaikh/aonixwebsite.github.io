About IOT

The internet of things (IoT) is a network that connects various types of devices, including computers, mechanical and digital machines, objects, animals, and even humans, each of which is assigned a unique identifier (UID) and has the capacity to exchange data with other nodes in the network independently of human intervention. Web-connected smart devices with embedded systems including CPUs, sensors, and communication gear make up what is known as a "Internet of Things" (IoT) ecosystem. Connecting to an IoT gateway or other edge device allows IoT gadgets to pool their collected sensor data, which can then be transferred to the cloud or analysed locally. These gadgets can share data with one another and take appropriate action based on the results of those conversations. Although humans can interact with the devices (to set them up, provide instructions, or access the data), the gadgets themselves do most of the work independently.
